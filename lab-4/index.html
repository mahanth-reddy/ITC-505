ABSTRACT

Traffic congestion is increasingly becoming a severe problem in many large cities around the world. The problem is more complex in developing countries where cities are growing much faster than those in the developed countries. With the increase of population, the traffic is also being increased day by day. It has become a hell kind of experience in these days to go out in the peak hours, mostly in the metropolitan cities. It takes hours to go to a near place where there have been number of traffic signal junctions. At present the traffic control system is following the timer system in which a particular time period is defined for the vehicles to move. By this we may not exactly clear the traffic, because the traffic may not be constant in all the directions. So here is the project to control the traffic depending on the density (i.e., more number of vehicles) at the signal junctions.

This project has been designed by using four IR sensors for four approaching roads at a signal junction. These IR sensors count the number of vehicles in each direction and give this information to the microcontroller. Depending on the information received, the controller takes the decision to give green signal to those particular direction vehicles whose density is much higher than other directions and allows them. And the rest of the signals will be in red for the duration of period defined in the controller. Likewise, the priority is given to that particular direction whose density is more and clears the traffic. Then the controller again checks the sensor inputs and will give signals appropriately depending on the density.

The emergency vehicles like ambulance, fire engine, etc the communication system installed in the vehicles can clutch the traffic at signals until they cross the signal post. The system is designed for junction/cross-roads, where often ambulance has to wait until the normal traffic is cleared. This is quite inconvenience for the patient who needs immediate treatment. There by this system is designed which can by-pass the existing signaling system temporarily.

As the embedded system can identify the approaching direction, except this way signal for the remaining three ways will be red, there by traffic will be clutched and way for the emergency vehicle will be cleared. During normal time these indicators remains in off condition, whenever the emergency vehicle is approaching from a specific direction, the driver has to activate the corresponding key from the transmitter. Likewise, all four sides’ traffic can be controlled according to the approaching direction of the emergency vehicle. After crossing the junction (signal post), simply by pressing reset key from the emergency vehicle, the signals are restored automatically. The same system can be implemented in VVIP vehicles also.

INTRODUCTION

The development of intelligent traffic control systems has become increasingly vital due to the burgeoning number of vehicles on the road and the perpetual need for safety and efficiency. Modern traffic engineering involves dynamic analysis and synthesis of automotive technologies to manage traffic systems effectively. In this context, the project emphasizes the creation of reliable control structures for urban and motorway traffic, leveraging advanced technologies like communication networks and sophisticated algorithms to optimize traffic light controllers. This approach aims not only to minimize waiting times for road users but also to provide them with information to navigate cities more efficiently, thereby reducing fuel consumption and environmental impact.

HARDWARE COMPONENTS USED

  • Atmel 89C51 and 89C2051 Micro Controller IC’s
  • LM567 Tone decoder
  • RF Transmitter and Receiver
  • Voltage Regulator IC (7805)
  • BC 547 NPN Transistors
  • Relay

The required PCB’S (Printed Circuit boards) for the project work fabricated by SUN RISE CIRCUITS, Kushaiguda Industrial Estate, Hyderabad. Kushaiguda Industrial Estate is very famous for fabricating the Industrial grade PCB’s. The project utilizes Atmel 89C51 and 89C2051 Micro Controller ICs for processing, LM567 Tone Decoder for audio signal detection, RF Transmitter and Receiver for wireless communication, Voltage Regulator IC (7805) for stable power supply, BC547 NPN Transistors for signal control, and Relays for switching high-power devices.
